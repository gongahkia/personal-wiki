    • A monoid (M,⋅,e)(M, \cdot, e)(M,⋅,e) is a set MMM equipped with a binary operation ⋅:M×M→M\cdot: M \times M \to M⋅:M×M→M and an identity element e∈Me \in Me∈M such that:

      • Identity: a⋅e=aa \cdot e = aa⋅e=a and e⋅a=ae \cdot a = ae⋅a=a for all a∈Ma \in Ma∈M

  5. Product and Sum Types

    • Product Types: Denoted by A×BA \times BA×B, represent pairs of elements from sets AAA and BBB.
    • Sum Types: Denoted by A+BA + BA+B, represent elements that belong to either set AAA or set BBB.

  6. Limits and Colimits

    • Limit: The limit of a diagram (a functor from an index category to C\mathcal{C}C) is an object that captures the idea of the “best approximation” to the diagram.
    • Colimit: Dual to limits, capturing the “coarsest” structure that can be mapped to from the diagram.

    • The category Fun(C,D)(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D})(C,D) consists of all functors from C\mathcal{C}C to D\mathcal{D}D and natural transformations between them.

    • A monad on a category C\mathcal{C}C consists of:

  10. Initial and Terminal Objects

    • Initial Object: An object III in a category C\mathcal{C}C such that for every object A∈CA \in \mathcal{C}A∈C, there exists a unique morphism from III to AAA.
    • Terminal Object: An object TTT in a category C\mathcal{C}C such that for every object A∈CA \in \mathcal{C}A∈C, there exists a unique morphism from AAA to TTT.

  11. Equivalence of Categories

    • Two categories C\mathcal{C}C and D\mathcal{D}D are said to be equivalent if there exist functors F:C→DF: \mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{D}F:C→D and G:D→CG: \mathcal{D} \to \mathcal{C}G:D→C such that G∘FG \circ FG∘F is naturally isomorphic to the identity functor on C\mathcal{C}C, and F∘GF \circ GF∘G is naturally isomorphic to the identity functor on D\mathcal{D}D.
